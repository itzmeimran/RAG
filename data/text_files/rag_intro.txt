Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that enhances the capabilities of large language models by combining them with external knowledge sources. Instead of relying only on pre-trained data, RAG retrieves relevant information from a document store and uses it to generate more accurate and context-aware responses. Frameworks like LangChain make it easier to build RAG pipelines by integrating document loading, text splitting, embeddings, and vector databases. This approach is widely used in applications such as question answering systems, chatbots, and knowledge assistants.
